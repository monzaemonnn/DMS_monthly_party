🏮 Matsuri Food & Machine Prep Guides

🍢 Yakitori (Sous-Vide & Torch)

The Method: Heat pre-cooked frozen skewers in a cooler bag using a Sous-Vide wand, then char with a blowtorch on demand for that smokey grilled texture without a charcoal mess.

  • Sous-Vide: Set to 65°C (149°F) for 1–2 hours to warm through completely.
  • Finishing: Lay on a baking sheet/tray, brush with tare (sauce), and blowtorch for 5–10 seconds per side until bubbled and charred.

🥢 Hashimaki (Chopstick Roll)

The Method: Savory okonomiyaki batter spread thin, toppings added, then rolled around wooden chopsticks on the flat griddle.

  • Batter: 100g okonomiyaki flour + 150ml water + 1 egg.
  • Steps: Pour batter in a long oval shape. Add tempura bits, red ginger, cheese/pork. When bubbles form, place two chopsticks at one end and roll up carefully.
  • Sauce: Drizzle sauce, Kewpie mayo, aonori, and bonito flakes.

🍗 Matsuri Karaage (Double Fry)

The Method: Double-fried Brazilian chicken thigh chunks for maximum crunch on the outside and juicy tenderness inside.

  • Marinade (Tonight): Garlic/ginger paste + soy sauce + sake + pinch of sugar. Cut chicken into 4cm pieces and marinate overnight.
  • Dredge: Coat thoroughly in Katakuriko (potato starch) right before frying.
  • Double Fry: 1st fry at 160°C (320°F) for 3 mins (rest for 4 mins). 2nd fry at 190°C (375°F) for 1 min until golden brown.

🌊 Nagashi Somen (Flowing Noodles)

The Machine: Circulating tabletop water slide. Keep it cold and flowing!

  • Prep: Boil somen for exactly 2 minutes. Shock immediately in ice water. Roll into small bite-sized nests.
  • Running: Fill machine with cold tap water and a generous amount of ice cubes. Drop noodles in small batches and let guests catch them.
  • Sauce: Serve chilled tsuyu in cups with grated ginger and chopped green onions.

🍧 Shaved Ice (Kakigori)

The Machine: Shaved ice machine. Standard cubes or blocks depending on motor type.

  • Ice: Freeze special molds tonight, or prepare bag ice from Donki.
  • Shaving: Shave fine, snow-like ice into paper bowls. Pat down gently.
  • Flavors: Pour syrup (melon/strawberry) and drizzle sweet condensed milk on top.

🧪 Neon Jello Shots (for funsies)

The Mix: Colorful neon gelatin shots using sweet juices and vodka.

  • Proportion: 1 pack gelatin (5g) + 120ml boiling water (dissolve) + 80ml vodka + 50ml bright juice (like Fanta Melon/Grape).
  • Pour: Pour into small plastic cups, cover with lids, and chill in the fridge for at least 4 hours.

🥞 Choco-Banana Crepes (Teppan Style)

The Method: Thin, sweet crepes cooked on the flat hotplate, loaded with whipped cream, banana slices, and chocolate syrup.

  • Batter: 100g flour + 2 eggs + 200ml milk + 15g melted butter + 1 tbsp sugar. Whisk and rest for 30 mins.
  • Cooking: Pour a thin layer on a low-medium hotplate, spread quickly. Flip after 1 minute, cook for 30 seconds more.
  • Toppings: Cool slightly, fold in half, spray whipped cream, add sliced banana, chocolate syrup, fold into a cone.

🍟 Nori-Shio French Fries (Deep Fry)

The Method: Golden, crispy shoestring French fries seasoned with salt and aonori seaweed flakes.

  • Fry: Deep fry frozen fries in hot oil (180°C / 350°F) for 4–5 minutes until golden and crisp.
  • Seasoning: Drain oil immediately, transfer to a paper bag or bowl, toss generously with fine salt and aonori flakes.
