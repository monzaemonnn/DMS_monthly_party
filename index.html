🏮 Matsuri Food & Machine Prep Guides

🍢 Yakitori (Sous-Vide & Torch)

Method: Warm frozen skewers in a cooler box with a Sous-Vide wand, then sear with a blowtorch on demand for that charred matsuri finish.

Ingredients:
  • Frozen pre-cooked Yakitori (50 skewers)
  • 1 bottle Yakitori Tare (sauce)
Steps:
  1. Pre-warm cooler box with water. Set Sous-Vide wand to 65°C.
  2. Submerge sealed bags of skewers for 1–2 hours to heat through.
  3. Arrange on baking tray, brush generously with tare.
  4. Blowtorch for 5–10s per side until bubbly and charred.

🐙 Takoyaki (Octopus Balls)

Method: Golden crispy batter balls filled with octopus pieces, cooked live on a hotplate well grid.

Ingredients (for ~40 balls):
  • 300g Takoyaki mix flour
  • 2 eggs & 900ml water
  • 200g frozen octopus (cut into bite-size)
  • Chopped green onions, tempura bits, red ginger
  • Takoyaki sauce, Kewpie mayo, aonori, bonito flakes
Steps:
  1. Whisk flour, eggs, and water into a thin, smooth batter.
  2. Oil the takoyaki griddle wells generously and heat up.
  3. Pour batter to overflow the wells. Drop octopus in each well.
  4. Scatter green onions, tempura bits (tenkasu), and ginger.
  5. As the edges set, cut the grid and flip 90° using a pick.
  6. Flip completely and rotate until golden-brown spheres.
  7. Serve hot topped with sauce, mayo, aonori, and bonito flakes.

🥢 Hashimaki (Chopstick Roll)

Method: Okonomiyaki batter spread thin, loaded with toppings, then rolled around chopsticks live on the griddle.

Ingredients:
  • 100g Okonomiyaki flour mix & 150ml water
  • 1 egg
  • Shredded cabbage, tempura bits, pickled red ginger
  • Slices of pork belly or cheese slices (optional)
  • Okonomiyaki sauce, Kewpie mayo, aonori, bonito flakes
  • Disposable wooden chopsticks (wari-bashi)
Steps:
  1. Whisk flour, water, and egg into a smooth batter.
  2. Heat flat griddle to medium-high and oil lightly.
  3. Spread a ladle of batter into a thin 15cm oval.
  4. Scatter cabbage, tempura bits, red ginger, and pork/cheese.
  5. Place chopsticks at one end and roll up pancake tightly.
  6. Press seam down to seal and brown. Top with sauces & aonori.

🍝 Yakisoba (Fried Noodles)

Method: Classic Japanese fried noodles sautéed with pork belly and cabbage on the flat teppan.

Ingredients:
  • 3 packs Yakisoba noodles
  • 150g pork belly slices
  • Shredded cabbage & chopped green onions
  • Yakisoba sauce (bottle)
  • Benishoga (pickled ginger) & aonori flakes
Steps:
  1. Heat flat hotplate and fry pork slices until browned.
  2. Add cabbage and sauté until slightly softened.
  3. Place noodles on top, pour 3 tbsp water to steam-loosen.
  4. Stir-fry noodles together, then drizzle sauce generously.
  5. Toss until sauce caramelizes. Top with benishoga and aonori.

🍗 Matsuri Karaage (Double Fry)

Method: Overnight marinated chicken chunks double-fried for ultimate crunch and juiciness.

Ingredients (for 2kg):
  • 2kg chicken thighs (cut into 4cm pieces)
  • 3 tbsp garlic paste & 3 tbsp ginger paste
  • 5 tbsp soy sauce & 3 tbsp sake & 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 cup Katakuriko (potato starch)
  • Deep-frying vegetable oil
Steps:
  1. Marinate chicken in garlic, ginger, soy, sake, sugar overnight.
  2. Coat chicken thoroughly in Katakuriko starch before frying.
  3. 1st Fry: Heat oil to 160°C. Fry for 3 mins. Drain and rest 4 mins.
  4. 2nd Fry: Heat oil to 190°C. Fry for 1 min until golden brown.

🌊 Nagashi Somen (Flowing Noodles)

Method: Chilled somen noodles sliding down a water track, dipped in cold tsuyu sauce.

Ingredients:
  • 400g dry somen noodles
  • 1 bottle somen tsuyu (dipping sauce)
  • Grated ginger, chopped green onions, sesame seeds
  • Ice cubes & cold water for the machine
Steps:
  1. Boil somen noodles in rolling water for exactly 2 minutes.
  2. Rinse under cold running water, rubbing off excess starch.
  3. Shock in ice water, drain, and roll into small nests.
  4. Fill somen machine with cold water and ice. Start pump.
  5. Drop noodle nests in stream; dip in tsuyu with condiments.

🍧 Shaved Ice (Kakigori)

Method: Fluffy shaved ice drizzled with sweet colorful syrups and condensed milk.

Ingredients:
  • Ice cubes / ice blocks
  • Kakigori syrups (strawberry, melon, blue Hawaii)
  • Sweetened condensed milk (squeeze tube)
Steps:
  1. Load ice into the shaving machine.
  2. Shave ice into paper bowls, shaping it gently into a dome.
  3. Pour syrups in colorful stripes over the shaved ice dome.
  4. Drizzle condensed milk generously over the top and serve.

🧪 Neon Jello Shots (for funsies)

Method: Sweet, colorful gelatin shots spiked with vodka, set in portion cups.

Ingredients:
  • 2 packs (10g) gelatin powder
  • 240ml boiling water
  • 160ml vodka
  • 100ml neon fruit juice (Fanta Melon/Grape)
  • Small plastic portion cups with lids
Steps:
  1. Dissolve gelatin powder completely in boiling water.
  2. Stir in vodka and neon fruit juice.
  3. Pour mixture into portion cups and cover with lids.
  4. Place in fridge for at least 4 hours until completely set.

🥞 Choco-Banana Crepes (Teppan Style)

Method: Soft, paper-thin sweet crepes cooked on the low-heat hotplate and filled with toppings.

Ingredients (for ~6 crepes):
  • 100g all-purpose flour & 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2 eggs & 200ml milk
  • 15g melted butter
  • Spray whipped cream can
  • 2 bananas (sliced) & chocolate syrup bottle
Steps:
  1. Whisk flour, sugar, eggs, and milk. Stir in melted butter. Rest 30m.
  2. Pour batter on a low-medium hotplate and spread very thin.
  3. Cook 1 min until edges curl, flip, cook 30s. Let cool.
  4. Fold in half, spray cream, add bananas & choco, fold twice.

🍟 Nori-Shio French Fries (Deep Fry)

Method: Crispy deep-fried potatoes tossed with fine salt and aonori seaweed flakes.

Ingredients:
  • 1kg frozen French fries
  • Deep-frying vegetable oil
  • Fine salt & aonori flakes
Steps:
  1. Heat frying oil to 180°C.
  2. Deep fry frozen potatoes for 4–5 minutes until golden.
  3. Drain immediately and transfer to a paper bag or bowl.
  4. Sprinkle salt and aonori flakes, shake vigorously to coat.

🥒 Kyuuri no Ippon-zuke (Cucumbers on Sticks)

Method: Whole chilled cucumbers lightly shaved, salted, marinated in dashi, and skewered for a refreshing festival snack.

Ingredients:
  • 8-10 Japanese cucumbers (Kyuuri)
  • 2 tbsp coarse salt (for rubbing)
  • 2 tsp dashi powder & 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 300ml water
  • Thick bamboo skewers / chopsticks
Steps:
  1. Wash cucumbers. Shave thin skin stripes lengthwise using a peeler.
  2. Rub cucumbers with coarse salt to remove bitterness, rinse.
  3. Boil water, dissolve dashi powder and soy sauce, then cool down.
  4. Place cucumbers in a Ziploc bag with cooled dashi brine, refrigerate for 4+ hours.
  5. Skewer each cucumber lengthwise with a bamboo stick and serve cold.

🍹 Lemon Sour Bar & Soft Drinks

Method: A self-serve drink station centered around classic Suntory Lemon Sour concentrate and ice-cold soft drinks.

Ingredients:
  • Suntory Lemon Sour Concentrate (1.8L)
  • Carbonated water (Soda) & Rock ice bags
  • Soft drinks (Coke, Oolong/Green Tea)
  • Lemon slices (optional garnish)
Steps:
  1. Fill a festival cup to the brim with rock ice.
  2. Pour 1 part Suntory Lemon Sour Concentrate (approx 45ml).
  3. Top with 3 parts carbonated soda water (approx 135ml).
  4. Stir gently once from the bottom to retain carbonation.
  5. For non-alcoholic options, serve chilled tea or cola over ice.

🏮 Matsuri Atmosphere & Setup

Method: Transform the sharehouse common room/balcony into a vibrant, glowing night market stall alley.

Setup Items:
  • Matsuri paper lanterns & fairy string lights
  • Disposable plates, bowls, cups, and chopsticks
  • Bluetooth speaker (Japanese festival music playlist)
Steps:
  1. Hang red and white paper lanterns along the walls and balcony.
  2. String up fairy lights around the food tables for a warm glow.
  3. Set up distinct cooking zones for the teppan hotplate, deep fryer, somen machine, and ice shaver.
  4. Queue up a traditional matsuri playlist (Taiko drum music or Awa Odori).
  5. Place disposable plates, skewers, and cups in an accessible station.
